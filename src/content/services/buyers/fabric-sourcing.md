### Starting with the right materials

Everything in garment manufacturing starts with the fabric. If you get the weight, the hand-feel, or the composition wrong at the start, the final garment will never be what you imagined. We don't just take the factory's word that they have "similar" fabric; we go to the mills and the markets to find the exact match for your design.

Bangladesh has a massive domestic textile industry, but knowing which mill excels at heavy fleece versus light linen is a job for local experts. We collect swatches, verify the GSM (grams per square meter), and check the shrinkage rates before a single meter of fabric is cut for your samples.

### Making your tech pack "factory-proof"

A tech pack is the blueprint for your garment. If there is an ambiguity in the stitching instructions or the measurement points, a factory will make their own best guess. Usually, their guess won't match your vision. We review your tech packs through the eyes of a production manager.

We look for the small details that cause big problems: Is the tolerance range realistic? Is the thread type specified for the seam strength? Is the placement of the care label compliant with your country's regulations? By fixing these issues on paper, we save you weeks of back-and-forth during the sampling stage.

### Cost-effective material choices

Sometimes, the fabric specified in a design is unnecessarily expensive or has a very high MOQ. We provide "spec reality checks." If we know a nearly identical fabric that is regularly stocked by local mills, we'll suggest it. This can often drop your unit price significantly and reduce your lead times, as the factory won't have to wait 30 days for a custom knit or dye run.
