### The "Black Box" of production

Once you approve the pre-production sample and the factory starts bulk, you often enter a "black box" period. You ask for updates, and you get a generic "Everything is on track, don't worry." You only find out the fabric was delayed by 10 days or the sewing line is moving slower than expected when the shipping deadline passes.

In Bangladesh, production schedules are tight. A delay of two days in the dye house can ripple into a two-week delay at the port. Monitoring production from an office in London or New York is impossible. You need someone who can see the fabric sitting on the warehouse floor and count the bundles being moved to the sewing lines.

### Proactive bottleneck identification

We don't just ask the merchandiser for a status update. We go to the floor and see the "WIP" (Work in Progress). We check the Daily Output Reports. If the factory promised to sew 1,000 pieces a day but they are only hitting 600, we find out why.

Is it a difficult seam that needs a specialized attachment? Is a machine down? By identifying these issues in the first three days of production, we can work with the factory to adjust the plan before the delay becomes unmanageable.

### Accurate reporting you can trust

Every week, you receive a concise report with photos of your actual garments on the line. You'll see the fabric being cut, the labels being sewn, and the first pieces moving into finishing. This level of transparency gives you peace of mind and allows you to manage your own customers' expectations with data, not guesses.
