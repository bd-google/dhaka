### Breaking the 5,000 piece barrier

The biggest frustration for a new clothing brand is the "Minimum Order Quantity" or MOQ. You find a factory you love, you show them your tech pack, and they tell you they won't touch anything under 3,000 or 5,000 pieces per color. For a startup, that is an impossible financial burden and a massive inventory risk.

Bangladesh is famous for its massive factories, but there is a growing sector of smaller, agile units. These factories are set up to handle 100, 200, or 500 pieces. They use the same high-quality yarn and the same skilled workers, but their business model is built on flexibility rather than raw volume.

### How we make small orders work

We maintain a database of these "small-run" specialists. These aren't middlemen; they are dedicated manufacturers who understand the needs of boutique brands. We don't just ask them to take your order; we manage the process to ensure it's profitable for them and affordable for you.

One way we do this is through fabric pooling. If three of our clients want black 180gsm cotton jersey, we can buy the fabric in bulk and split it across the three orders. This keeps the material cost down even if your individual order is small.

### Quality over quantity

Just because an order is small doesn't mean the quality should suffer. In fact, smaller factories often have better oversight because the owner is frequently on the floor. We apply the same rigorous inspection standards to a 100-piece order as we would to a 10,000-piece shipment.

Our goal is to help you grow. We find the factory that fits you today, so that when you are ready to order 5,000 pieces, you already have a trusted partner who knows your brand and your quality expectations.
