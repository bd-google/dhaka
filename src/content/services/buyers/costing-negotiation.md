### Understanding the "Gharment" math

When a factory gives you a price of $4.50 for a hoodie, do you know how they reached that number? For most buyers, the quote is a "black box." They don't know how much is for the fabric, how much is for the labor, and how much is just "padding" because the factory isn't sure if you'll be a difficult client.

In Bangladesh, costing is a science. It's based on "consumption" (how much fabric is used per piece) and "SMV" (Standard Minute Value—how many minutes it takes to sew). Because we speak the language of the factory, we can pull apart a quote and see where the hidden costs are.

### We negotiate from a position of data

We don't just "ask for a discount." That doesn't work. Instead, we challenge the specifics. We might say, "Your fabric consumption is calculated at 1.8 meters, but for this size chart, it should be 1.6 meters." Or, "The trim cost you've listed is 30% higher than the current market rate in Dhaka."

When the factory sees that we know the local market prices for yarn, dyeing, and labor, the "foreigner's premium" disappears. They give us their best, most realistic price because they know they can't hide behind vague numbers.

### Maximizing your margin

Our goal is to maximize your profit margin without compromising the quality of the garment. Sometimes, we suggest small design changes—like moving a seam or changing a pocket style—that save 5 minutes of sewing time. This can drop your cost by $0.20 or $0.30 per piece. On a 1,000 piece order, that pays for our service and puts extra profit in your pocket.
