### Why the sampling process breaks down

The biggest delay in fashion manufacturing isn't the bulk production; it's the sampling phase. You send a tech pack, wait two weeks, and receive a sample that is three sizes too small with the wrong collar shape. You send comments, wait another two weeks, and the next sample is better but still not right.

This happens because the person reading your tech pack is often a merchandiser, not the pattern maker. Details get lost in translation. The pattern maker might follow a "standard" block they use for other clients instead of your specific measurements. Without someone in the room to clarify your needs, you waste months in a "sample loop."

### Direct sample room management

We bridge that gap. When your sample is being developed, we don't just wait for the courier. we visit the sample room. We talk to the pattern makers and the sample machinists. We check the first "muslin" or "proto" before it's even finished to catch obvious errors in proportion or construction.

If you are using a specific wash—like a heavy enzyme wash for denim or a soft silicone wash for t-shirts—we oversee the trial. We ensure the factory accounts for the shrinkage that happens during these processes so your final bulk garments actually match your size chart.

### Speeding up "Approval for Bulk"

Our goal is to get you to "Gold Seal" or "Pre-production (PP) Sample" approval in two rounds or less. We provide you with detailed reports, photos of the garment on a form, and a verified measurement table before we even mail the sample to you.

By the time the physical sample reaches your desk, you already know it fits. This confidence allows you to move to bulk production weeks earlier, ensuring your collection hits the shelves (or your online store) on schedule.
