### Quality is not an accident

In a high-speed production environment, mistakes happen. A needle breaks and leaves a hole. A tension setting is slightly off, causing a puckered seam. A worker misses a spot during the final press. If these errors aren't caught in the factory, they become "returns" and "bad reviews" for your brand.

Many factories have their own internal QC (Quality Control), but their priority is hitting the shipping deadline. Our priority is your reputation. We act as an independent third party, answerable only to you.

### Two-stage inspection process

We recommend a two-stage approach to quality. First, the **Inline Inspection**. We visit when about 20-30% of the order is sewn. This allows us to catch "systemic" errors—like a pocket being placed 1cm too low—before the whole order is finished. It's much easier to fix a mistake before the garment is fully assembled.

Second, the **Pre-Shipment Inspection (PSI)**. Once the order is 100% finished and at least 80% packed, we return for a final check. We pull random cartons from the stack, open them, and inspect the garments inside. We check measurements, stitching, fabric cleanliness, and even the accuracy of the hangtags and polybags.

### The Inspection Report

Within 24 hours of the inspection, you receive a detailed report with clear "Pass" or "Fail" results. We include high-resolution photos of any defects found—classified as Critical, Major, or Minor. You have the final say. If you aren't happy with the quality, the factory doesn't ship. This is your ultimate leverage to ensure you get exactly what you paid for.
