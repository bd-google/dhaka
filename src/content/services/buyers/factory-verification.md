### The risk of the "hidden middleman" in Bangladesh

It's common for small brands to find a supplier online who looks perfect. The website shows a massive factory, and the person on WhatsApp is fast and polite. You pay the 30% deposit for samples or bulk, but then things slow down. Messages go unanswered. Delays happen every week.

Often, this is because you aren't talking to a factory. You're talking to a commission agent who is still trying to find a small factory that will take your order. They don't own the machines. They don't control the quality. When the real factory gets a bigger order, your job gets pushed to the back of the line.

### We go where you can't

We act as your eyes on the ground. When you find a potential partner, don't send money until someone has walked through the gate. Our team visits the factory unannounced or by appointment to see who is actually sitting at the sewing machines.

We count the lines. We check if they actually make the product they say they do. If you want t-shirts, but the factory is full of heavy denim machines, that is a red flag. We take our own photos—not the staged ones from a brochure—so you see the real conditions of the production floor.

### Verification of legal and safety standing

Beyond the machines, we check the paperwork. We verify that the factory has a valid trade license and the necessary export permits. We also look for safety compliance. In Bangladesh, being "ready for export" means meeting specific safety standards. We check if they are part of the relevant industry bodies.

By the time we finish our audit, you will have a clear report. You'll know if they have 50 machines or 500. You'll know if they are a legitimate manufacturer or just an office with a laptop. This small step prevents the biggest mistake in sourcing: sending money to the wrong person.
