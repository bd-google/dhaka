### Protecting your revenue in Bangladesh

For many foreign suppliers, the "Bangladesh risk" is all about getting paid. While the vast majority of factories are honest and reliable, the banking and L/C (Letter of Credit) process in Bangladesh is notoriously slow and bureaucratic. Discrepancies on documents can hold up payments for weeks, and some buyers use these small errors as leverage to delay payment.

Emails from an accounting department in another country have very little impact. A local person walking into the factory's finance office has a lot of impact.

### We are your "Commercial Department"

We don't just wait for a payment to become "overdue." We track the process. Did the factory submit the documents to their bank? Has the bank sent the "acceptance" message? By monitoring the "paper trail" locally, we identify problems before they lead to a payment delay.

If a dispute arises—for example, if a buyer claims a quality issue to avoid paying the full invoice—we act as a mediator. We visit the factory to see the "defects" for ourselves. Often, we find that the issue is minor and can be resolved with a small credit or a promise for the next order, allowing the main payment to be released immediately.

### Respectful but firm follow-up

Our approach is professional and relationship-focused. We aren't a "debt collector." We are your local partner. We understand the factory's constraints, but we also ensure they respect yours. This firm, consistent, in-person presence is the best way to ensure your cash flow remains healthy in the Bangladesh market.
