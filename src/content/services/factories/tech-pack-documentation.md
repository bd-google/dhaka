### When the paperwork is the problem, not the product

A lot of shipment disputes aren't actually about quality. They're about a tech pack that didn't specify tolerance ranges clearly, a compliance file that was formatted differently than the buyer's checklist expected, or a measurement point that meant one thing to your pattern maker and another thing to the buyer's QC inspector. The garment can be exactly right and still get flagged, because the paperwork around it didn't communicate that clearly.

### Turning what you know into what buyers expect to see

Your factory already has the technical knowledge — the patterns, the construction details, the compliance records. The gap is usually in how that information gets presented in English, in the format a buyer's team is used to reviewing. We take what you already have and restructure it: clear tolerance ranges, consistent measurement points, properly labeled compliance documents.

This also means building templates you can reuse. Once a tech pack format is buyer-ready for one order, the same structure works for the next one, cutting down the back-and-forth that usually happens with a new buyer relationship.

### Fewer disputes, faster sign-offs

The goal isn't just paperwork for its own sake — it's fewer rejected shipments and faster payment releases, because the buyer's QC team isn't stopping to ask what a spec actually meant.
