### Why capable factories still lose orders

More than 100 Bangladeshi garment factories closed in 2024, and Vietnam overtook Bangladesh as the world's second-largest apparel exporter in 2025. In most of these cases, it wasn't the sewing machines or the workers who were the problem. Buyers consistently say they stay with suppliers who respond quickly, communicate clearly, and act professional — and quietly move on from ones who don't.

A factory owner or manager already has a full day running production. Answering a detailed buyer email in polished English, at the speed a buyer expects, competes for time with everything else on the floor. Something has to give, and usually it's the email.

### What a buyer-facing representative actually does

We sit between your production team and the buyer's inbox. When a buyer asks a technical question, we know enough about garment construction to either answer it directly or get the right answer from your team fast, then translate it back into the tone and format a buyer expects.

We track every open conversation — sample requests, price negotiations, shipment questions — so nothing goes quiet for a week because everyone assumed someone else was handling it. Buyers notice the difference immediately: replies come the same day, in clear English, from one consistent contact instead of a rotating cast of people.

### Built around your existing team, not replacing it

This isn't about taking over your factory's relationships. It's about giving your production team a buffer so they're not context-switching between the cutting table and an English-language email negotiation. You stay in control of pricing and production decisions — we just make sure buyers get the fast, professional response that keeps them from looking elsewhere.
