### Too many acronyms, not enough clarity

BSCI, WRAP, GOTS, OEKO-TEX, Sedex/SMETA — for a factory that's never gone through a certification process, these can look interchangeable. They're not. Each one is built for a different kind of buyer requirement, a different market, and a different set of standards, and getting the wrong one wastes time and money without opening the doors you actually wanted opened.

### Matching certification to buyers, not the other way around

We start by asking which buyers and markets you're actually trying to reach, then work backward to the certification that matches. A factory chasing European fast-fashion buyers has different priorities than one targeting US big-box retail. Getting this match right the first time avoids paying for a certification that doesn't move the needle with your target buyers.

### An honest look before the real audit

Before you commit to a formal certification audit, we do a plain-language gap assessment: where your current practices already meet the standard, and where they don't. This means no surprises during the real audit, and a realistic sense of the work and cost involved before you start.

We don't issue certificates ourselves — that has to come from an accredited third-party body, and we'll refer you to the right one. Our role is making sure you walk into that process prepared, targeting the certification that actually matters for the buyers you want.
