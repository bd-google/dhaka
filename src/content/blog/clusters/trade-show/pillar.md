### The Chaos of a Dhaka Trade Show

If you’ve ever been to the ICCB (International Convention City Bashundhara) during a textile expo, you know it is high-energy, crowded, and loud. Thousands of factory owners and merchandisers move through the halls. It is a fantastic opportunity, but it is also very easy to feel overwhelmed.

Most foreign companies make the same mistake: they fly in two people, stand in a booth for four days, collect 500 business cards, and then fly out. They feel successful, but a card in a box isn't a sale.

### Pre-Show: The Targeted Invite

The real winners at a trade show start working 30 days before the doors open. They don't wait for people to "walk by." Instead, they use their local representative to reach out to specific "Target Accounts" (the top-tier factories they really want to work with) and invite them for a private demo at the booth.

### At the Show: Bridging the Language Gap

While English is the language of business in Bangladesh, the nuances of a technical machinery sale or a complex fabric performance often get lost. Having a local assistant who can speak Bengali to the factory's production manager while you speak English to the owner is a game-changer. It ensures that the "technical" people and the "financial" people are both happy.

### Post-Show: The 48-Hour Rule

This is where sales are won or lost. In the two weeks after a trade show, every visitor you met will be contacted by ten other suppliers. If you wait until you get back to your home country to follow up, you are too late.

A local support team can visit the most promising leads the very next day. While the conversation is still fresh, they can bring the physical samples or the technical quotes the buyer asked for. This physical presence—visiting the factory in person—shows a level of commitment that no email can match.

### Conclusion

Trade shows in Bangladesh are an investment in time and money. To get a return on that investment, you need a local strategy that starts before the show and continues long after you've flown home.
