### The cost isn't just fuel

A factory switching to generator power during outages faces an immediate cost increase — one Chattogram factory reported daily fuel costs rising from around Tk19,000 to Tk40,000 as outages lengthened. But there's a slower, less visible cost too: sustained generator use causes voltage fluctuations that damage sensitive machinery over time, shortening equipment lifespan in a way that doesn't show up on this month's invoice but affects reliability and output quality later.

### Why diesel isn't always a dependable backup either

During the most acute phase of the current regional energy disruption, diesel supply itself has faced rationing at the national level, with factories unable to obtain the quantities they need even when willing to pay for it. A backup plan that assumes diesel is always available when the grid fails hasn't held up consistently through this period.

### What this means for evaluating a factory right now

A factory's generator capacity and diesel supply arrangement is a reasonable, specific thing to ask about directly if your order timeline is tight — not because it's unusual to rely on backup power currently, but because how well-prepared a specific factory is for sustained outages varies, and it's a fair predictor of how much your specific order is likely to be affected.
