### Why "newly built" isn't automatically a good sign right now

A brand-new, modern facility sounds like a positive signal — better equipment, cleaner compliance profile, more capacity. But reporting has identified a genuinely striking pattern: some fully constructed Bangladeshi industrial facilities, including major, well-capitalized groups, have sat waiting years for a gas connection to actually begin production, with tens of billions of taka in investment effectively idle in the meantime.

### Why this happens

Bangladesh's industrial energy model has historically depended heavily on gas, both for direct heat/power use (boilers) and for captive power generation. New industrial zones and facilities need a gas connection approved and installed before they can operate at their designed capacity, and that approval process has, in a meaningful number of documented cases, stalled for years across multiple government agencies without resolution.

### What this means when evaluating a newer factory specifically

If a factory is relatively new or recently expanded, confirming their actual, current gas/power connection status — not just their stated capacity — is worth doing directly rather than assuming a modern facility means a fully operational one. A factory's marketing materials describing its capacity reflect what it's designed to do, not necessarily what it's currently able to do.
