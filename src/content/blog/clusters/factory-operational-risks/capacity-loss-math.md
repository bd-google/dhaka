### Why this isn't just "a bit slower"

A factory quoting a delivery date based on its normal production capacity, while actually running at 40-60% of that capacity due to power shortages, isn't facing a minor slowdown — it's facing close to double the calendar time to produce the same volume, if the constraint doesn't ease. A documented case saw one factory's daily output fall from 60 tonnes to 10 tonnes — not a 20% dip, a collapse to roughly a sixth of normal output.

### Why factories don't always volunteer this clearly

A factory in the middle of this pressure is also trying to retain your business, and admitting a severe capacity shortfall upfront risks losing the order to a competitor — even if that competitor is under the same regional constraint. This creates an incentive to quote based on hoped-for normal capacity rather than current actual capacity.

### What actually gets you an honest number

Asking directly and specifically about current power situation and realistic current daily output, not just "can you meet this date" — and treating a confident yes without detail as a reason to ask more specific questions, not less, given how widespread this constraint currently is.
