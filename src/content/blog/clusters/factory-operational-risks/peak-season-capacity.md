### Why this period is so heavily booked

Fall/winter retail season and major Western holiday shopping periods drive a large share of global apparel buyers to place orders on a similar calendar — meaning Bangladesh's factories face genuinely compressed demand roughly August through December, with production slots booked well in advance by buyers who plan for this every year.

### Why this catches newer buyers off guard specifically

An established buyer with a long-standing factory relationship typically has a production slot reserved based on history and volume commitment. A newer buyer approaching a factory for the first time during this exact window is competing for whatever capacity is left over — meaning both timeline and pricing flexibility are worse during peak season than the same factory would offer in a quieter month.

### What this means for planning

If your product timeline allows any flexibility, placing orders outside the August-December crunch — or confirming and booking a production slot well ahead of it if your calendar requires this window — meaningfully reduces both cost and delay risk. For a first order specifically, entering during peak season without an existing factory relationship is one of the more avoidable ways to end up with the capacity-overpromising problem covered in our <a href="/blog/capacity-overpromising">dedicated piece</a> on that exact issue.
