### Why this is easy to miss

A buyer typically approves a fit sample in one size — often a middle size like medium — and assumes the factory's grading (scaling that pattern correctly up and down across the full size range) is accurate. If the grading has an error, every size except the one you actually fit-tested can be off, and nobody notices until customer returns or complaints start clustering around specific sizes.

### Why it happens

Grading is a technical pattern-making step, separate from the base pattern itself, and errors here don't show up in a spec sheet review — they only show up in a physical garment. A factory rushing a new style, or one less experienced with your brand's specific fit block, is more likely to introduce a grading inconsistency than an established, careful pattern room.

### What actually catches this before bulk production

Requesting fit samples in more than just your sample size — at minimum the smallest and largest sizes in your range, not just medium — before approving grading for the full run. This is a small additional sampling cost that's far cheaper than a bulk order with a systematic fit problem across several sizes.
