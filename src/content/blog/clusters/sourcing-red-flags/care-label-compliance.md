### Why this is a market-specific trap

Care labeling symbols, required language, and country-of-origin marking rules vary by destination — what's compliant for an EU shipment isn't automatically compliant for the US or another market. A factory with deep experience serving one market can carry that market's labeling habits over to an order for a different market without realizing the requirements changed.

### Why this is a bigger problem than it sounds

A labeling error isn't always caught until the shipment reaches customs at the destination, at which point correcting it means unloading, relabeling, or in some cases rejection and return — all considerably more expensive and slower than catching it before goods ship.

### What actually prevents this

Explicitly stating your destination market's specific labeling requirements in your tech pack, rather than assuming a factory will apply the correct standard by default — and requesting a label proof for approval before bulk production, the same way you'd approve a fabric swatch or a color reference.
