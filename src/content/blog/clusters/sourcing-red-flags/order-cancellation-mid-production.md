### The scale this can reach

During major global demand shocks, Bangladeshi factories have faced waves of order cancellations after production was already underway or complete — leaving factories holding finished or partially-finished goods, raw material costs already sunk, and workers already paid, with no buyer left to ship to. Industry advocacy at the time pushed major brands to publicly commit to paying in full for in-production and completed orders specifically because this pattern isn't rare during periods of demand shock.

### Why this isn't just a "force majeure, nobody's fault" situation

A purchase order without clear cancellation terms tied to production stage leaves a factory with no contractual protection once a buyer decides to walk away — even where the buyer's own market conditions, not the factory's performance, caused the cancellation.

### What actually protects a factory here

Contract terms that specify cancellation obligations tied to production stage — for example, requiring payment for raw materials already purchased and labor already committed if cancellation happens after a certain point — rather than an open-ended cancellation clause with no cost attached to the buyer regardless of how far along production is.
