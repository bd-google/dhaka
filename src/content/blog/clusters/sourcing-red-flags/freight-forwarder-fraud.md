### Why this stage gets overlooked

Most sourcing due diligence focuses on the factory — registration, capability, compliance. The freight forwarder or shipping agent handling goods after they leave the factory is a separate party, with separate risk, and it gets far less scrutiny by comparison. A documented case saw roughly 30 Bangladeshi garment exporters defrauded through a single local freight forwarding agent — a reminder that the chain of trust doesn't end at the factory gate.

### What this actually looks like

Documentation or payment irregularities at the logistics stage — shipments that don't reach their stated destination, forwarders who aren't properly licensed or bonded, or payment collected for freight services that aren't actually rendered as agreed.

### What actually reduces this risk

Using established, verifiable freight forwarders with a real track record rather than the cheapest available option, and keeping visibility into shipment tracking independently rather than relying solely on the forwarder's own updates.
