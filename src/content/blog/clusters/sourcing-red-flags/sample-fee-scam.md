### The pattern

A buyer finds an attractive listing — often on a general B2B marketplace rather than an industry-specific one — makes contact, and is asked to pay a sample fee or courier charge before receiving anything. The payment goes through. Communication slows, then stops, or the "sample" that arrives doesn't match what was discussed at all.

### Why this is easy to miss

Legitimate sample fees are completely normal in this industry — factories genuinely do charge for samples, especially to unfamiliar first-time buyers, to filter out non-serious inquiries. The scam version is indistinguishable at the request stage; it only becomes obvious after the money moves and nothing follows.

### What actually distinguishes a legitimate request from a scam

A real factory has a checkable trade license, BIN, and physical address independent of what they tell you directly — verify these before paying anything, not after. A generic B2B marketplace listing with no independently verifiable registration details, unusually fast pressure to pay, and payment requested to a personal rather than business account are the three signals worth treating as a stop sign.
