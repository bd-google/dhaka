### Why this catches first-time buyers off guard specifically

Eid-ul-Fitr and Eid-ul-Adha bring extended factory closures and a broader slowdown in the weeks around them, as workers travel and factories plan reduced schedules — a well-known fact to anyone who's sourced from Bangladesh before, and an easy thing to miss entirely for a first-time buyer building a timeline from a factory's quoted "production time" without asking about the calendar.

### Why it's not just the closure days themselves

The disruption isn't limited to the holiday days on the calendar — factories often run at reduced capacity in the lead-up as workers prepare to travel, and it takes time to return to full production pace afterward. A deadline calculated by simply subtracting the holiday dates from your timeline usually still underestimates the real impact.

### What actually accounts for this

Asking a factory directly whether your production window overlaps with Eid or other major holidays before finalizing a delivery date, and building in extra buffer time on either side of the closure, not just for the closure days themselves.
