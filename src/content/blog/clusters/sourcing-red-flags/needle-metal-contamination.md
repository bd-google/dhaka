### Why this happens

Industrial sewing needles break during normal production — it's a routine occurrence given the number of needle passes involved in garment construction, especially on heavier fabric or dense stitching. The risk isn't that a needle breaks; it's a broken fragment ending up inside a finished garment undetected, particularly one intended for a child (see our <a href="/blog/kidswear-sourcing">kidswear-specific compliance piece</a>) or any product where an embedded metal fragment poses real injury risk.

### Why this is a bigger liability question than it first appears

A metal fragment reaching a customer isn't just a quality complaint — it's a product safety incident, with real legal and reputational exposure for the brand, regardless of how rare the underlying cause is across a large production run.

### What actually prevents this

Metal detection equipment that every finished unit passes through before packing — standard practice at established factories, and worth confirming directly as an actual running process (not just equipment sitting unused) rather than assuming it happens because the factory owns a detector.
