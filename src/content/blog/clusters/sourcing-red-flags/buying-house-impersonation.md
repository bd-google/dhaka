### Why this layer is a risk point

Buying houses (intermediaries who place orders on behalf of a brand, or represent factories to foreign buyers) are a completely normal, legitimate part of Bangladesh's garment trade. That normalcy is exactly why a fraudulent one can operate for a while without detection — a factory or buyer dealing with an intermediary has less direct visibility into who's actually on the other end than they would dealing with a counterpart directly.

### What a documented case looked like

In a reported large-scale fraud case, the two buying houses involved in placing fraudulent orders were confirmed by the Bangladesh Garment Buying House Association to not be members of their organization — a detail that would have been checkable before the orders were placed, not just after the fraud came to light.

### What actually checks this

Confirming an intermediary's standing with the relevant industry association (BGMEA, BGBA, or similar) independently, rather than taking their self-description at face value — and being more cautious, not less, when an unusually attractive order arrives through an unfamiliar intermediary rather than a known one.
