### What this actually looks like

A professional-looking website, product photos that look legitimate (often copied from a real factory's site or catalog), fabricated or borrowed certification logos, and pricing that looks attractive relative to the market. Building this costs very little and takes very little time — which is exactly why it's worth remembering that a good-looking website is not itself evidence of anything.

### How one specific case got caught

In a documented instance, a buyer about to place a bulk order engaged a sourcing agent, who found discrepancies in the factory's stated address and license number. A physical audit revealed a front operation using another factory's photos — caught before the order was placed, saving the buyer an estimated $40,000.

### What actually would have caught it earlier

Checking the trade license number and registered address against Bangladesh's actual public/official records, independent of what the website or the sales contact states, before any payment or production commitment — not as a formality after you've already decided to work with them.
