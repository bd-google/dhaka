### How this actually works

A buyer believes they're dealing with a factory directly, or with a transparent sourcing agent charging a clear service fee. In practice, some intermediaries quietly mark up the factory's real price by a hidden percentage, presenting the inflated number as the "factory price" with no disclosed commission at all — meaning the buyer has no way to know how much of what they're paying is production cost versus undisclosed margin.

### Why this is different from a normal, legitimate agent fee

A transparent sourcing relationship discloses its fee structure — a flat fee, a percentage, or a markup stated openly — so a buyer can evaluate whether the total cost is fair. An undisclosed markup removes that ability entirely, and makes it impossible to know whether you're getting a competitive price or simply paying whatever margin the intermediary decided to add.

### What actually protects against this

Asking directly whether you're dealing with the factory itself, and if not, what the intermediary's fee structure is — a legitimate agent will disclose this without resistance. Getting an independent quote or price sanity-check from a source other than the party presenting the "factory price" is the most direct way to confirm whether a quote reflects genuine production cost.
