### What actually happened

Bangladesh's garment sector came under a formal US Trade Representative review over alleged counterfeit clothing exports bearing US and French brand names. Industry representatives pointed to a specific mechanism: leftover or surplus stock from legitimate production runs sometimes gets sold into secondary markets, and from there can end up mislabeled or associated with brands that never authorized it — without the original factory necessarily being complicit in the counterfeiting itself.

### Why this matters to a legitimate buyer, not just to enforcement bodies

This is a reputational and legal exposure question for buyers licensing designs, logos, or brand names to be produced in Bangladesh: what happens to fabric cuttings, rejected units, and surplus stock from your own production run matters, because uncontrolled leftover product is exactly the raw material this diversion pattern uses.

### What actually reduces this exposure

Confirming a factory's stated policy and practice for handling rejected units, fabric surplus, and production overage — including whether excess/rejected stock is destroyed, returned, or sold, and under what oversight — as part of your ongoing quality and compliance relationship, not as an afterthought.
