### Why this happens

A factory wants your business, and a confident "yes, we can deliver by that date" is a more appealing answer than a cautious one — especially for a new buyer relationship a factory is trying to win. The gap between that answer and the factory's actual current order backlog and line capacity isn't visible from a sales conversation.

### Why it's hard to catch from outside

A buyer has no independent way to see a factory's other commitments or how full its production lines currently are — you're relying entirely on the factory's own account of its capacity, from the same people who have an incentive to say yes to your order.

### What actually surfaces this before it becomes a missed deadline

Asking directly about current order volume and how your order would fit into the existing production schedule, not just whether the date is theoretically possible. A factory visit that includes actually observing the production floor — not just a meeting room — gives a much more honest picture of real current capacity than a verbal commitment does.
