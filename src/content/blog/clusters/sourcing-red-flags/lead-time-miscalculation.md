### The gap between "production time" and "your actual date"

When a factory quotes "4 weeks production," that's usually the sewing-floor time for your order once everything else is ready — it doesn't include fabric/yarn lead time, sampling rounds and approval delays, waiting for an open production slot, or the shipping and customs time after goods leave the factory. A knitwear order realistically running 12-18 weeks end-to-end, or a woven/denim order running 90-130 days, can look like a much shorter commitment if you only ask about the sewing stage specifically.

### Why this catches buyers off guard specifically on a first order

An established factory relationship has a track record to sanity-check quoted timelines against. A first order doesn't — and factories, wanting to win new business, don't always volunteer the full realistic timeline unprompted.

### What actually closes this gap

Ask specifically for a stage-by-stage timeline — fabric/yarn sourcing, sampling and approval rounds, production slot availability, sewing and finishing, and shipping — rather than accepting a single "production time" number. Build a realistic buffer into your own retail or launch calendar rather than planning against the most optimistic number you were quoted.
