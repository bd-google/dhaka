### Why this is a real, recurring factor

Bangladesh's garment sector has periodically experienced worker protests over wages and conditions, sometimes disrupting production across a region for days at a time. This is a genuine operational risk to factor into planning, not a one-time historical event — the underlying pressures (wage negotiations, cost-of-living conditions) resurface periodically.

### Why overreacting is also a mistake

The industry's track record shows production returning to normal operation after these episodes, generally within a predictable window, not a sustained collapse. A buyer who treats every reported incident as a reason to abandon a factory relationship entirely may be reacting to headline severity rather than the industry's actual recovery pattern.

### What realistic planning looks like

Building a production calendar with buffer time around periods historically associated with wage-negotiation cycles, rather than assuming zero disruption risk. Avoiding total dependence on a single factory or a single industrial region for a critical, time-sensitive order, so a regional disruption doesn't stop your entire supply chain at once.
