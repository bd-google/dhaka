### Why this fails silently

A garment can pass every visual quality check — correct measurements, clean stitching, right color — and still fail once it's actually washed, if dye fixation or fabric treatment wasn't properly tested. Color bleeding, shrinkage beyond spec, or a garment that loses its shape after one wash cycle are invisible at the point of shipment and only surface in the customer's hands.

### Why standard inspection sometimes misses this

A pre-shipment inspection that checks measurements, construction, and visual appearance doesn't automatically include a wash test, because it requires actually laundering a sample and waiting — a step some inspection processes skip under time pressure, especially on a fast turnaround order.

### What actually catches this before shipment

Requiring a wash-test result (colorfastness to washing, shrinkage, and durability through a specified number of cycles) as a standard part of your quality approval process, not an optional extra — particularly for any fabric using dyes or finishes you haven't used with this specific factory before.
