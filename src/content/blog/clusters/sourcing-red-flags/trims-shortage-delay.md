### Why this is an underrated delay risk

Buyers tracking production progress tend to watch the big milestones — fabric arrival, cutting, sewing — and pay less attention to small trim components (buttons, zippers, specific labels, elastic) that are individually cheap but can stop a finished garment from actually being finished if one is missing or delayed. A factory that's 95% done can still miss a ship date entirely over one outstanding trim component.

### Why it happens

Trims are often sourced from a different supplier than the main fabric, sometimes on a shorter lead-time assumption that doesn't hold up if that supplier has its own delay. Custom trims (a branded button, a specific zipper pull) carry more of this risk than generic stock items a factory can substitute easily.

### What actually prevents this from becoming a surprise

Confirming trim sourcing and lead time as part of your production timeline review, not just fabric — and for custom trim components specifically, building in a realistic buffer or a pre-approved generic substitute option if the custom item runs late.
