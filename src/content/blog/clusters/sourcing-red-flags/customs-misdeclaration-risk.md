### Why buyers assume this isn't their problem

Export paperwork is prepared on the Bangladesh side, so it's easy to assume any documentation error is entirely the factory's or forwarder's liability. In practice, once goods arrive at the destination port, customs authorities hold the importer of record accountable for what's declared — regardless of who actually filled out the paperwork.

### What this actually looks like

An understated declared value (sometimes intended to reduce duty), a misdescribed product category, or missing/incorrect country-of-origin documentation can trigger a hold, an audit, penalties, or in serious cases seizure — consequences that land on the receiving buyer, not just the shipping factory.

### What actually reduces this exposure

Reviewing shipping documentation yourself before goods ship, rather than trusting it was handled correctly without checking — and working with factories and forwarders who have a track record of clean, accurate customs paperwork, since this is exactly the kind of thing that's hard to fix retroactively once a shipment is already in transit or held.
