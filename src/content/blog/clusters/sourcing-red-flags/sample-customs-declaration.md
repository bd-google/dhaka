### Why suppliers do this

A small fabric swatch or trim sample genuinely has low commercial value, and it's tempting to declare it as close to zero as possible to avoid duty and paperwork friction on something that isn't actually being sold. This isn't usually meant as fraud — it's an attempt to simplify what feels like an oversized process for a small item.

### Why it often backfires

An unrealistically low declared value, especially paired with a vague product description, is one of the specific patterns customs screening flags for manual review — the opposite of the fast, simple clearance the low value was meant to achieve. What was meant to speed things up ends up being the reason a small parcel sits for review instead of clearing quickly.

### What actually works better

A realistic, defensible declared value with a specific, accurate commercial description ("cotton fabric swatch, sample only, no commercial value" as description with a small but honest nominal value) clears more predictably than an unrealistically low number that reads as evasive to a customs reviewer.
