### The real risk of sourcing in Bangladesh

If you're reading this, you've likely found a potential supplier on Alibaba or through a LinkedIn message. They've sent you beautiful photos of a clean, modern factory and promised you the best prices. But in Bangladesh, what you see on a screen is often very different from the reality on the ground.

The "middleman factory" is the biggest risk for small and medium-sized brands. These are agents who present themselves as manufacturers but don't own a single sewing machine. They take your deposit, take a commission, and then scramble to find a small, low-quality factory to sub-contract your order to. You lose control of quality, and your delivery date is never guaranteed.

### Step 1: Verify the Trade License and IRC/ERC

Every legitimate manufacturer in Bangladesh must have a Trade License, an Import Registration Certificate (IRC), and an Export Registration Certificate (ERC). Don't just ask if they have them—ask for a scan of the current year's renewal.

Check the name on the license. If the name of the company you are paying doesn't match the name on the factory's license, you are dealing with an agent. Also, look at the address. If the license is for a 5th-floor office in downtown Dhaka but the "factory" is in Gazipur, something is wrong.

### Step 2: The Video Call Test (Unannounced)

A simple way to filter out the most obvious middlemen is to ask for a live video call. Don't schedule it for tomorrow—ask for it now. Ask them to walk from their desk to the production floor.

If they make excuses—"the floor is closed for lunch," "the manager is away," or "WiFi doesn't work there"—it's a massive red flag. A real factory owner or merchandiser can walk onto the floor at any time. Look for the machines. Are they making garments similar to yours? Do the workers have uniforms or ID cards that match the company name?

### Step 3: Check Industry Body Membership

Most export-oriented garment factories in Bangladesh are members of the **BGMEA** (Bangladesh Garment Manufacturers and Exporters Association) or the **BKMEA** (Bangladesh Knitwear Manufacturers and Exporters Association).

These organizations maintain member directories. You can search for the factory name on their websites. Membership doesn't guarantee quality, but it does mean they are a legally recognized entity in the sector. If a "factory" claims to be a major exporter but isn't in these databases, walk away.

### Step 4: Third-Party Physical Verification

The only way to be 100% sure is to have someone physically walk through the factory gates. They need to count the sewing lines, check the safety exits, and verify the machines. A professional inspector will check the "WIP" (Work in Progress) to see if the factory is actually capable of handling your specific fabric and construction.

### Conclusion: Trust, but verify

Bangladesh offers incredible value and skill, but it requires local oversight. By following these steps, you protect your brand's capital and your reputation. Don't send a single dollar of deposit until you know exactly who is on the other side of that bank transfer.
