### Spotting the "Hidden Agent"

In the Bangladesh garment industry, middlemen are everywhere. While some provide value as coordinators, many pretend to be the factory itself to secure a higher margin. This lack of transparency is where the risk begins.

Here are the most common signs you are talking to a middleman:

1. **The "Too Many Products" Red Flag**: If a factory claims they can make heavy denim, high-stretch activewear, and delicate silk dresses all in the same facility, they are almost certainly a middleman. Real factories specialize in specific machine setups.
2. **Generic Email Addresses**: If the "factory owner" is using a @gmail or @outlook address instead of a company domain, it's a sign of a small-scale agent without an established infrastructure.
3. **No Live Video Tours**: They will send you high-quality pre-recorded videos, but they will always have an excuse for why they can't do a live video call from the production floor right now.
4. **Vague Address Details**: They will tell you the city (e.g., "Gazipur"), but they won't give you the exact building number and floor until the last possible second.
5. **Bank Account Mismatch**: If the bank details they provide for the deposit are for a company name that is different from the "factory" name, you are paying an agent's commission, not a manufacturer's invoice.

Before you send any money, ask for a copy of the Trade License and verify the address. If you're still unsure, have a local partner like Checkloom conduct a physical "Spot Check" visit.
