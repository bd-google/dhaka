### Bangladesh isn't just "basic knits" anymore

For a long time, Bangladesh's reputation was built on basic cotton knitwear — t-shirts, polos, simple fleece. That's changed more than most buyers researching the country realize. Specialist factories now run genuine technical activewear production: compression leggings, seamless tops, moisture-management performance gear built for actual athletic use, not just gym-adjacent basics with a sportier cut.

### The technical requirements that separate activewear from basic knitwear

Activewear fabric has to do specific jobs a basic cotton tee doesn't:

- **Moisture-wicking** — polyester and nylon blends engineered to pull sweat to the fabric surface for fast evaporation, rather than absorbing and holding it the way cotton does
- **Four-way stretch and shape retention** — higher-elastane (spandex/Lycra) percentages so leggings and compression wear don't sag or lose fit after repeated wear and washing
- **Seamless construction** — genuine seamless activewear uses circular knitting machines to produce garments with zero side seams, reducing friction points; this is a distinct, more specialized capability than standard cut-and-sew knitwear
- **Flatlock stitching** — the standard construction for compression wear specifically, because it lies flat against skin and reduces chafing in a way a standard seam doesn't

### Not every factory that lists "sportswear" actually specializes in it

This is the single most important filter for this category: a general knit factory can sew a moisture-wicking polo without any real difficulty, but compression leggings, seamless tops, and true technical performance wear require specific machinery (circular seamless knitting, bonding, laser-cutting for panel work) and fabric sourcing relationships most general factories don't have. Ask directly for previous work samples in the specific sub-category you need — compression leggings, running tights, or seamless tops — rather than accepting "we do sportswear" as a capability claim on its own.

### Realistic MOQ for activewear

Commonly **700 to 1,000 pieces per color/style/fabric combination** at factories genuinely set up for OEM technical production — broadly in line with general knitwear MOQ, though custom-dyed technical fabrics can push this higher. Some factories offer a stock "activewear color card" specifically to bypass high custom-dye minimums for smaller brands — worth asking about directly if your volume is on the lower end.

### Why "the sample was perfect, the bulk shipment wasn't" happens more here

This complaint comes up across garment sourcing generally, but it's a genuinely bigger risk with activewear specifically. High-elastane compression fabric and seamless knit construction don't behave with the same predictability at full production scale that a basic cotton jersey does — stretch recovery, seam integrity under tension, and shade consistency across a technical fabric are all harder to hold steady across a large run than they are across a small sample batch, unless the factory runs disciplined in-line QC checkpoints specifically calibrated for that fabric type, not a generic AQL check built for basics.

### What to actually verify before committing

- Direct evidence of prior work in your specific sub-category (compression, seamless, running/technical), not a general "sportswear" claim
- Whether seamless construction, if you need it, is run in-house on circular knitting machines or subcontracted
- QC checkpoints specific to technical fabric behavior (stretch recovery, seam integrity under tension) — not just a standard AQL inspection built around basics
- Relevant certifications for technical/synthetic fabric — OEKO-TEX for chemical safety, GRS if recycled-content claims matter to your brand
