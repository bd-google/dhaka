### The "Big Factory" Myth

The common wisdom is that Bangladesh is only for the "big players"—the H&Ms and Gap's of the world who order 50,000 pieces per style. While it's true that the massive "composite" mills dominate the skyline, there is a thriving ecosystem of small-scale manufacturers that specialize in low-MOQ (Minimum Order Quantity) runs of 100 to 500 pieces.

For a startup brand, these small factories are the key to scaling without going bankrupt on inventory. But finding them is the hard part. They don't have massive booths at international trade shows, and they often don't show up on the first page of Google.

### Why small orders are difficult (and how to fix it)

To a factory, a 100-piece order is a logistical challenge. It takes the same amount of time to set up the sewing line and the cutting table for 100 pieces as it does for 1,000. This is why the unit price is higher.

The secret to getting a "Yes" from a small factory is to make your order as easy as possible for them. Use "stock" fabric colors that the factory already has on hand. Keep your construction simple. If you want a custom-dyed fabric for only 100 t-shirts, you will almost certainly be rejected or charged a massive premium.

### Where to look for low-MOQ partners

Don't look in the massive industrial zones like Ashulia or Gazipur, which are built for high volume. Instead, look for units in the "commercial-residential" hubs like Uttara or Mirpur in Dhaka. These areas are home to many smaller "sampling and production" houses.

These units are often started by former production managers from the big factories who wanted to strike out on their own. They have the technical skill of the big players but the flexibility of a small business. They are often more willing to work with you on development because they hope to grow with your brand over time.

### The "Fabric Pooling" Strategy

One of the best ways to place a small order is through an agent who practices "fabric pooling." If an agent has five clients all wanting black 100% cotton hoodies, they can buy 500 pieces' worth of fabric at a bulk price and split it across the five brands. This gives you the price of a medium-sized order with the flexibility of a small one.

### Summary for Startups

1. **Be realistic**: Expect to pay a higher unit price for the privilege of a low MOQ.
2. **Be flexible**: Use materials and colors that are already available in the local market.
3. **Be present**: Either visit the factory yourself or use a local partner to ensure your small order isn't being pushed to the back of the line for a larger client.

Bangladesh is open for small business. You just need to know which door to knock on.
