### It's about "Setup Time," not "Sewing Time"

To a buyer, 50 t-shirts feels like a lot of work. To a factory with 1,000 workers, it's less than 15 minutes of production. But the "Setup Time" for those 50 pieces is the same as for 5,000.

1. **Pattern and Marker**: The pattern maker must still spend hours grading and laying out the marker.
2. **Machine Adjustment**: The thread tension and needle types must be set for your specific fabric.
3. **Fabric MOQ**: No mill will dye 10kg of fabric for a 50-piece order. The factory has to find "leftover" fabric, which may not match your quality standards.

To get a small order accepted, you must solve these three problems for the factory. Suggest using "stock colors," offer to pay a "sampling surcharge" to cover the setup time, and be prepared for a longer lead time as the factory will wait for a "gap" in their larger production lines.
