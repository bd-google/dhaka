### The machine is only as good as the person running it

A machinery or technical equipment supplier can do everything right — good product, fair price, responsive support — and still lose a repeat customer over one thing: the factory's operators never learned to run the equipment at its actual capacity. This is a quieter failure mode than a broken part, because the factory usually doesn't complain loudly; they just quietly decide the machine "wasn't worth it" and don't come back for the next one.

### What training coordination actually involves from abroad

Coordinating operator training when you're not physically in Bangladesh means a few concrete things happening in sequence: a demo/installation visit scheduled around your team's availability and the factory's production calendar, training materials that work for the specific machine and the specific operators (language and literacy-level appropriate, not just a translated manual), and a follow-up mechanism for the questions that come up in the first few weeks of real use, not just the first day.
