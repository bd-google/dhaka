### The uncomfortable documented fact behind this

Two factories operating inside the Rana Plaza building had passed BSCI audits before the 2013 collapse that killed over 1,100 workers. This isn't cited to dismiss social auditing entirely — it's cited because it's the clearest documented proof that passing an audit and being genuinely safe are not the same claim, and buyers who treat "they're audited" as a complete answer are relying on a system with real, acknowledged limits.

### Why "audit fatigue" is a real, named industry problem

Academic and industry research since Rana Plaza has specifically documented "audit fatigue" — factories subjected to multiple, often duplicative audits from different buyers and compliance initiatives in parallel, sometimes with different auditors reaching different conclusions about the same factory. Research has also found that auditors are frequently paid by the factories they're auditing, a documented conflict of interest, and that some audit reports have been found to recycle stock language across different factories rather than reflecting genuinely factory-specific findings.

### What this means practically

An audit report is a real, useful data point — not a complete guarantee. Industry initiatives like SLCP (Social & Labor Convergence Program) exist specifically to create a shared, harmonized assessment multiple buyers can rely on instead of each commissioning a separate, duplicative audit — worth knowing about if you're trying to reduce audit burden on a factory you work with regularly, or evaluating whether a factory's existing audits genuinely cover what matters to you specifically, rather than assuming any audit report is equivalent to any other.
