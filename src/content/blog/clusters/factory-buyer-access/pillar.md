### Buyers are doing the same due diligence you'd want them to do

If you're a Bangladeshi factory trying to win new export buyers, it helps to think about this from the buyer's side first: a serious international buyer has almost certainly been burned before, or knows someone who has. Building a convincing-looking company online costs very little — which means the buyer's first pass through any list of factories is mostly a filtering exercise, looking for reasons to eliminate options quickly. A legitimate, capable factory that doesn't present verifiable specifics gets filtered out right alongside the ones that deserve to be.

### Where buyers actually go looking

There isn't one channel — serious buyers typically cross-check more than one:

- **Industry directories** — BGMEA's own factory directory, Fibre2Fashion, and similar B2B platforms. Buyers use these specifically because the listing is tied to a verifiable association membership, not just a claim.
- **Trade shows** — events like the Dhaka Apparel Summit and Bangladesh Denim Expo remain a major channel, particularly for buyers who want to see samples and meet people before committing.
- **Referrals and sourcing agents** — a large share of serious, larger orders move through a referral or an agent who's already done the vetting, rather than cold outreach in either direction.
- **Direct outreach after a directory search** — but by the time a buyer reaches out directly, they've usually already screened out most of the list based on what was publicly checkable.

### What buyers are actually screening for, in the order they check it

1. **Is this a real, currently active company?** — trade license, BIN, RJSC registration, or at minimum a specific factory address, not just a company name and a contact form
2. **Is it a member of the relevant industry association?** — BGMEA or BKMEA membership is one of the fastest things a cautious buyer checks first, because it's independently verifiable rather than a claim on your own site
3. **Does it actually do what I need?** — specific product category and capability (knitwear vs. woven vs. denim), not a generic "we produce all types of garments"
4. **What compliance does it hold, specifically?** — named certifications (BSCI, WRAP, GOTS — see our <a href="/blog/compliance-certification">compliance certification breakdown</a>) rather than a vague "we follow international standards"
5. **Can I actually reach a specific person?** — a named contact with a title, not a generic info@ inbox

### The gap between what factories show and what buyers are checking for

Most factory websites and directory listings lead with photos of the building and general claims about quality and experience. That's not wrong, but it's answering a question buyers aren't really asking at the screening stage. A buyer scanning ten factory listings in one sitting isn't evaluating your building — they're looking for the specific, checkable details in the list above, and moving on quickly if they're not there.
