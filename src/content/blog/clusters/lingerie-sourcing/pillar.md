### This is a specialization, not an extension of basic garment production

It's a mistake to treat lingerie as "smaller, more delicate basics." Bangladesh's intimate apparel sector has specifically evolved from general garment production into specialized manufacturing — the country now hosts factories that supply lingerie specifically to major global retailers, and this shift reflects real investment in construction technique, materials sourcing, and workforce training, not an incremental step up from t-shirt production.

### The construction difference that actually matters

A basic garment is flat-sewn. A molded or padded bra involves a different manufacturing step entirely — heat-molding cups to a three-dimensional shape, a process that requires specific machinery and technique a general apparel factory doesn't necessarily have. This is the single clearest signal of whether a factory genuinely specializes in lingerie or is taking on an unfamiliar category: ask directly about molded cup production capability, not just general "intimate apparel" experience.

### The materials list is longer and pricier than basics

Lingerie draws on a wider fabric range than most categories — lace, satin, microfiber, spandex/lycra, mesh, and specialized narrow-fabric elastics for waistbands and straps. Several of these (lace and elastic specifically) are supplied domestically by specialist narrow-fabric manufacturers, but raw material price volatility in this specific materials basket is a genuine, recognized challenge for the sector — something worth factoring into cost expectations rather than assuming price stability across a season.

### QC checks specific to this category

Because these garments sit against skin for extended periods, quality checks go further than a standard garment inspection: pH testing (to catch anything that could irritate skin), perspiration and washing fastness, elasticity and recovery testing (to confirm elastic components hold their shape through repeated wear and washing), and fabric density/GSM verification. A factory unfamiliar with lingerie specifically may not run these checks as standard practice.

### Realistic MOQ

Lingerie MOQ runs a noticeably wider range than basics — commonly **500 to 5,000 pieces per style** — reflecting the real range in construction complexity from a simple brief to a fully molded, multi-component bra. Smaller specialist factories are often more willing to work at the lower end for developing brands than large-volume composite factories are.
