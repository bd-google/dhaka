### Why small orders need more QC, not less

Many small brands assume that because their order is only 200 pieces, "nothing can go wrong." In reality, small orders are often placed in smaller, less-automated factories where human error is more common. A 5% defect rate on a 200-piece order means 10 angry customers. For a startup, that is a reputational crisis.

Quality is not about "trusting" the factory. It is about **verifying** the output against a set of objective standards.

### The AQL Standard vs. 100% Inspection

For large orders, the industry uses **AQL (Acceptable Quality Limit)**, a statistical sampling method. However, for orders under 500 pieces, we often recommend a **100% Piece-by-Piece Inspection**.

During a 100% inspection, every single garment is checked for:
* **Stitching**: No skipped stitches, open seams, or raw edges.
* **Measurements**: Verified against the approved size chart.
* **Fabric Defects**: No holes, stains, or color shading within the garment.
* **Trims**: Buttons must be secure, zippers must move smoothly, and labels must be straight.

### The Independent Perspective

Internal factory QC teams are under pressure to "pass" the order so the factory can get paid and meet the shipping deadline. Their interests are not always aligned with yours.

An independent, third-party inspection provides an unbiased report. We have no incentive to hide defects. We provide you with high-resolution photos of every issue we find, categorized by severity (Critical, Major, Minor). You make the final decision: "Ship" or "Repair."

### Conclusion

Quality control is the insurance policy for your brand. By investing in an independent on-site inspection, you ensure that the garments reaching your customers are exactly what you promised them. In the competitive world of fashion, quality is your strongest marketing tool.
