### Why this gets missed

Government cash incentive schemes for qualifying garment and textile exports exist specifically to support the sector's competitiveness, but claiming them requires specific documentation tied to each shipment, submitted through a defined process with real deadlines. A larger factory with a dedicated compliance/documentation team claims this routinely as part of standard operations; a smaller factory without dedicated administrative capacity often doesn't, simply because nobody owns the process.

### Why this matters more now, not less

Given the real cost pressure covered in our <a href="/blog/factory-operational-risks">factory operational risks piece</a> — rising energy costs, currency-driven working capital squeeze — incentive money a factory is legally entitled to but isn't claiming is a real, avoidable gap in an already tight margin environment.

### What actually closes this gap

Assigning clear ownership of the incentive application process to a specific person or role, rather than leaving it as an occasional task nobody is accountable for, and building the required documentation into your standard export paperwork process from the start rather than trying to reconstruct it after the fact.
