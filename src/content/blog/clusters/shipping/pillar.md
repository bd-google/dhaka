### The Last Hurdle: The Paperwork

You've successfully managed the production and the quality control. Your goods are ready to ship. But for many buyers, the journey ends in frustration at the customs office. Why? Because of a simple typo on a piece of paper.

Garment logistics is a game of precision. The information on your Commercial Invoice must match the Packing List, which must match the Bill of Lading, which must match the Certificate of Origin. If there is even a small inconsistency, customs authorities will flag the shipment.

### Common Error 1: HS Code Mismatch

Every garment category has a Harmonized System (HS) code. A 100% cotton knit t-shirt has a different code than a synthetic blend hoodie. Using the wrong code can mean you pay the wrong import duty, leading to fines and delays. Always verify the HS codes with your local customs broker before the factory generates the documents.

### Common Error 2: Weight and Quantity Discrepancies

The "Net Weight" and "Gross Weight" on your packing list must be accurate. If the weight recorded at the port of Chittagong is significantly different from what is written on the documents, the container will be pulled for a manual inspection. This can add a week to your timeline.

### Common Error 3: Improper Description of Goods

Customs officers aren't fashion experts. They need clear, plain descriptions. Instead of "The Summer Breeze Top," the invoice should say "Women's 100% Cotton Woven Blouse." This clarity helps your goods move through the automated clearing systems faster.

### How to avoid these mistakes

1. **The Pre-Draft Review**: Ask the factory to send you "Draft" copies of all shipping documents before they print the final versions or send them to the bank.
2. **Independent Verification**: Have a local partner (like Checkloom) cross-check the packing list against the actual carton count we saw during the final inspection.
3. **Use a Local Freight Forwarder**: They understand the specific requirements of the Chittagong port and can often catch errors before the goods are even loaded onto the ship.

### Conclusion

Logistics delays are almost always preventable. By spending an extra hour reviewing the paperwork in Dhaka, you can save weeks of waiting and thousands of dollars in port fees at your destination.
