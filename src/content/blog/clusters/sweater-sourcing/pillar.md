### Bangladesh is the world's second-largest sweater manufacturing base, after China

This isn't a minor claim — industry coverage has specifically noted Bangladesh building the largest sweater manufacturing ecosystem outside China, and more notably, jacquard sweater production (the more intricate, patterned end of the category) has been actively shifting to Bangladesh from China, driven by rising Chinese labor costs and a shortage of skilled operators for the linking (finishing/seaming) stage of sweater production specifically.

### Different machine, different factory specialization

This is the detail most buyers researching sweaters for the first time miss: sweaters aren't made on the same circular knitting machines behind t-shirts and basic jersey. They're made on **flat knitting machines** — computerized equipment from brands like Shima Seiki, Stoll (favored for heavier gauges), and Cixing (favored for finer gauges). A factory that's excellent at t-shirts and hoodies isn't automatically equipped for sweaters — it's a genuinely separate machine investment and skill set, which is why Bangladesh's sweater manufacturing tends to concentrate in factories that specialize in it specifically, sometimes as a dedicated unit within a larger group.

### Gauge numbers work backwards from what you'd guess

Gauge refers to the number of needles per inch on the knitting machine, and it's counterintuitive on first encounter: a **lower** gauge number (3, 5, 7) means fewer, more widely-spaced needles, producing a coarser, chunkier, heavier knit — think a heavy cable-knit sweater. A **higher** gauge number (12, 14) means more closely-spaced needles, producing a finer, lighter knit — think a fine merino-style sweater you could wear under a jacket. Getting this backwards when briefing a factory is a fast way to receive a sample nothing like what you pictured.

### Yarn and finishing options worth knowing

Sweaters commonly use cotton, wool, acrylic, or blended yarns, and finishing/washing techniques specific to this category include acid wash, cool dye, stone wash, enzyme wash, and piece dye — each producing a different hand-feel and visual finish on the same base knit. Jacquard technique (multi-color patterned knitting) is where Bangladesh's recent competitive shift from China is most pronounced, so if your product involves patterned or intarsia work, it's worth asking a factory directly about their jacquard-specific experience and machine capability, not just general sweater production.

### Realistic MOQ

Commonly **500 to 1,000 pieces per style/color** at established sweater-specialist factories — broadly in line with general knitwear, with some factories offering flexibility toward the lower end for brands testing a new design.
