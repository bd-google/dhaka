### Why a Liaison Office?

As your business in Bangladesh grows, you will reach a point where having an "agent" is no longer enough. You need your own employees, your own branding on the door, and the ability to sign local contracts for things like office space and internet.

A Liaison Office (also known as a Representative Office) is the most popular legal structure for foreign textile and machinery companies. It allows you to have a permanent base in Dhaka without the complexity of a full Subsidiary or a Branch Office.

### The BIDA Process: A Checklist

The **Bangladesh Investment Development Authority (BIDA)** is the primary government body you will deal with. The process is rigorous and requires a significant amount of documentation from your home country.

1. **Application**: Submission of the official BIDA form with a detailed business plan for the Bangladesh market.
2. **Board Resolution**: A formal document from your company's board in your home country authorizing the setup.
3. **Company Documents**: Certificate of Incorporation, Memorandum and Articles of Association (all must be notarized and attested by the Bangladesh Embassy in your country).
4. **Bank Reference**: A letter from your home bank confirming your company's financial standing.

### The "6th Floor" Meeting

Once your application is submitted, it is reviewed by an inter-ministerial committee. They look for one thing: Will this office help the Bangladesh economy? For textile companies, the answer is almost always "Yes," as you are supporting the country's largest export sector.

You may be called for a hearing to explain why you need the office. Having a local partner (like ThreadBridge BD) to help prepare you for this meeting is invaluable.

### Post-Registration Compliance

Once you have your BIDA permission, you aren't done. You still need to:
* Register with the **RJSC** (Registrar of Joint Stock Companies).
* Obtain a **TIN** (Tax Identification Number).
* Get a **Trade License** from the local City Corporation.
* Open a local **Bank Account** and remit the required operating capital.

### Conclusion

Setting up a liaison office is a clear signal to the Bangladesh market that you are here to stay. It builds immense trust with factory owners. While the bureaucracy can be challenging, the long-term benefits of having a legal "home" in Dhaka far outweigh the initial setup costs.
