### Welcome to the World's Garment Hub

Bangladesh is a land of incredible opportunity for clothing brands. It offers a unique combination of skilled labor, vertical integration (local mills), and competitive pricing. But for a first-time buyer, the market can feel overwhelming and opaque.

This guide is designed to take you from your first "sketch" to your first "shipment" with confidence.

### Step 1: Understand the "Total Landed Cost"

The price the factory gives you (usually "FOB Dhaka" or "FOB Chittagong") is not your final cost. You must calculate your **Total Landed Cost**, which includes:
* **FOB Price**: The cost of the garment.
* **Shipping**: Freight costs and insurance.
* **Duty/Tax**: Import duties based on your country's trade agreements with Bangladesh (many countries offer duty-free access for garments).
* **Local Logistics**: Getting the goods from the port to your warehouse.

Knowing this number early ensures that your brand remains profitable.

### Step 2: The Importance of the Tech Pack

In Bangladesh, your Tech Pack is your contract. If it isn't in the tech pack, it won't be on the garment. Be extremely specific about measurements, fabric weight (GSM), trim types, and label placements. Avoid vague terms like "high quality" or "soft feel"—use technical specifications instead.

### Step 3: Finding a Partner, Not Just a Factory

Don't just go for the cheapest quote. Look for a factory that is interested in your brand's growth. A factory that is willing to spend time on your development and answer your questions is far more valuable than one that saves you $0.10 per piece but ignores your emails.

### Step 4: The Golden Rule: Inspect Everything

Never ship a first order without an independent, third-party inspection. This is the single most important step to protect your brand. Even the best factories can make mistakes. Catching them in Dhaka allows the factory to fix them; finding them in your warehouse 30 days later is a disaster.

### Conclusion

Bangladesh is open for business. By being prepared, being realistic about costs and timelines, and having a local partner to act as your eyes on the ground, you can put the country's manufacturing scale to work for your brand instead of getting lost in it.
