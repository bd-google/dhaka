### A genuinely strange gap, once you notice it

Bangladesh is the world's second-largest garment exporter — a genuinely massive, sophisticated production base serving nearly every major global apparel brand. And yet, industry commentary has specifically pointed out that the country still hasn't built a strong, unified digital platform connecting global buyers directly with its producers, leaving most individual factories dependent on traditional, in-person channels — trade shows, referrals, and established relationships — for a business that increasingly starts with an online search.

This isn't a criticism of the industry's capability. It's a mismatch between production sophistication and how that capability gets presented to a buyer who's never set foot in the country.

### Why the standard "digital marketing" playbook doesn't quite apply here

Most general digital marketing advice assumes a consumer audience — social media engagement, brand storytelling, visual content for a scrolling feed. Bangladesh's garment sector is overwhelmingly B2B, and in a B2B sourcing context specifically, direct email outreach and structured follow-up outperforms consumer-style social content as a lead-generation channel. A factory investing in an active Instagram account while under-investing in a clear, specific email process for inbound buyer inquiries has the priority backwards for this industry.

### What buyers are actually checking online, before any conversation happens

This connects directly to what we've covered in <a href="/blog/factory-buyer-access">how buyers actually screen factories</a>: a website with clearly stated, current compliance certifications and specific product capability genuinely influences a buyer's decision before direct contact starts. The gap isn't usually "no website" — it's a website that leads with generic claims and building photos instead of the specific, checkable details a buyer doing a first-pass screening is actually looking for.

### Where the real channels are, for factories building this out

- **A B2B-focused website**, not a consumer-brand-style site — leading with certifications, specific capability, and verifiable registration details
- **Direct email outreach and follow-up**, treated as the primary channel it actually is in this industry, not an afterthought to social media
- **Presence on the B2B platforms buyers actually use** — Fibre2Fashion, industry-specific directories, and the BGMEA's own listings, rather than general business directories with no sector relevance
- **Case studies and specific work examples**, which carry more weight in a sourcing decision than general brand storytelling
